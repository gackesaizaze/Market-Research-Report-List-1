<p><p>The Gallium Nitride (GaN) power transistor market is primarily categorized into two types: Gallium Nitride Silicon Carbide (GaN-SiC) transistors and Gallium Nitride Silicon (GaN-Si) transistors. GaN-SiC transistors combine the properties of both materials, offering high efficiency, thermal conductivity, and robustness, making them suitable for high-power applications. In contrast, GaN-Si transistors leverage the advantages of Gallium Nitride on a silicon substrate, delivering impressive performance for medium-power applications while maintaining cost-effectiveness for consumer electronics and renewable energy systems.</p></p>
