<p><p>The Unattended Noise Monitoring market includes two primary types: wired and wireless systems. Wired noise monitoring involves physically connecting sensors to a centralized system, offering stable performance and continuous data collection, ideal for permanent installations. Conversely, wireless noise monitoring uses technologies like Bluetooth or Wi-Fi, allowing for flexible deployment and easier access to remote locations. This adaptability makes wireless systems suitable for temporary setups or areas where cabling is impractical, providing real-time insights into noise levels.</p></p>
